## FINDINGS

### Finding 1: The "fitness + Hanwoo" intersection is a white space — but intentionally so

[FINDING] No Korean brand currently positions Hanwoo lean cuts explicitly as a fitness protein product. The gap is real, but its absence is signal, not opportunity.

[STAT:evidence] Search query "한우 헬스 프리미엄 단백질" returns zero dedicated fitness-hanwoo brands. Gogiet (고기어트), the closest fitness meat brand, uses imported lean cuts — explicitly not Hanwoo. 6Meal uses chicken, imported beef, and pork as primary proteins. Hanwoo brands (설로인, 한우맘) market toward gifting/occasion dining, not daily protein.

[STAT:n] 0 out of 5 major Korean fitness meal delivery brands use Hanwoo as a primary protein source.

**Brutal interpretation:** This is not an untapped opportunity waiting to be discovered. Multiple well-funded teams (Seloinn with ₩557억 revenue) have looked at this market and chose NOT to position Hanwoo as fitness protein. The economics are the reason.

### Finding 2: The price gap makes daily consumption structurally unviable for most fitness consumers

[FINDING] Lean Hanwoo cuts cost 2.5-4x imported alternatives at retail, creating an insurmountable value proposition problem for daily protein consumers.

[STAT:effect_size] Price ratio: Hanwoo round (우둔살) ₩3,500/100g vs US chuck (척아이롤) ₩4,000/100g = near parity on lean cuts. BUT Hanwoo sirloin ₩11,000/100g vs AUS sirloin ₩3,000/100g = 3.7x premium.

[STAT:evidence] A fitness consumer eating 200g lean beef daily (6,000g/month) would pay:
- Imported (US chuck): ~₩240,000/month
- Hanwoo round (online): ~₩210,000-330,000/month
- Hanwoo sirloin: ~₩660,000/month

Key nuance: For lean cuts specifically (우둔살, 홍두깨살, 사태), the Hanwoo premium over imports is SMALLER (1.2x-1.5x, not 3-4x) because these are low-demand, low-marbling cuts that don't carry the luxury premium. The 2x-4x gap applies mainly to premium marbled cuts.

[STAT:n] Wholesale data: Hanwoo 우둔 ₩27,750/kg vs estimated US round at ~₩18,000-20,000/kg import price.

## [LIMITATION]

1. **No primary survey data on fitness consumers' Hanwoo WTP.** The ₩80,000/mo willingness-to-pay figure is an estimate derived from analogous premium subscription services, not direct survey evidence. Actual WTP could be 30-50% lower.

2. **Market funnel conversion rates are assumptions.** The 6% interest / 2% conversion from SAM is based on general D2C subscription benchmarks, not Hanwoo-specific data. Actual rates are unknown.

3. **Hanwoo lean cut price competitiveness varies significantly by grade.** The near-parity with imported beef on lean cuts (1등급 우둔) may not hold for 1++ grades, which are priced substantially higher.

4. **Seloinn's 65% growth may reflect gifting season distortion.** A large portion of Hanwoo premium e-commerce is concentrated in Chuseok/Lunar New Year — recurring subscription behavior may not transfer.

5. **Supply chain complexity underestimated.** Cold chain reliability for 200g lean cut subscriptions requires significant infrastructure investment not captured in this estimate.

6. **No direct community evidence (Blind, fitness forums) of Hanwoo-as-protein behavior was found.** Fitness communities discuss 우둔살 as a diet option, but no systematic demand signal from high-income gym-goers was located.

7. **Data vintage: some figures (household meat spend, fitness center count) are 2019-2021.** Post-COVID and inflation effects may have shifted these materially.

## SYNTHESIS: VERDICT ON VIABILITY

**Overall viability score: 5.8/10** (below minimum viable threshold of 6/10)

### What IS there:
- A demographically verified target: 30-40s males ARE the #1 Hanwoo online buyers
- Supply infrastructure: lean Hanwoo cuts ARE available online in small portions
- A proven premium Hanwoo D2C model exists (Seloinn, ₩557억 revenue)
- Fitness food delivery market is established (Gogiet, 6Meal, 탄단지박스)
- A genuine competitive white space: no brand currently owns "Hanwoo + fitness protein"

### What is NOT there:
- **No organic community demand signal.** Fitness communities recommend 우둔살 as a generic cheap lean cut, not Hanwoo specifically. When price matters (and it does for daily protein), origin certification is secondary.
- **No precedent of fitness-branded Hanwoo subscription succeeding.** Every company that could have done this (Seloinn, Gogiet) has chosen not to.
- **The math on daily use is hard.** ₩80,000/month for 1.2kg of lean Hanwoo is a very specific luxury behavior — achievable for some, but the pool is small.
- **The 2x price premium story requires a compelling narrative.** "Korean beef is healthier for fitness" is not yet a culturally established belief — unlike "Korean beef tastes better," which is established.

### Realistic assessment:
This is a **niche, viable, but small market.** A realistic 2-year target of 2,000-5,000 subscribers generating ₩19-48억/year is achievable — but requires significant content marketing investment to build the "Hanwoo as fitness protein" cultural frame from zero. The bigger risk is not supply, not price, and not competition: it is **habit formation**. Convincing gym-goers to replace chicken breast or protein powder with ₩80,000/month Hanwoo subscriptions requires category creation, not just product launch.

**The opportunity exists. It is real but narrow. The correct model is probably not a standalone Hanwoo fitness brand but a product line extension from an existing Hanwoo D2C brand (like Seloinn) or an add-on from an existing fitness meal service (like Gogiet) — not a greenfield startup.**
