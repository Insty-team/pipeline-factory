# Narrow Hypotheses for Tomorrow

Date: 2026-04-03
Purpose: resume quickly tomorrow without reopening broad strategy.

## Working frame
We are **not** testing a broad “AI marketing for beauty” idea.
We are testing a very narrow service wedge first.

## Narrow target
Korean-market medical aesthetics clinics with public laser hair removal pricing or treatment pages.

### First two live targets
1. Muse Clinic Myeongdong
2. Shinebeam Clinic Gangnam

---

## Narrow product definition
### Short-term service model
A concierge service that:
1. takes official clinic URLs
2. extracts only source-backed facts
3. turns them into a provider-approved canonical record
4. publishes an AI-readable public page
5. tracks before/after discoverability signals

### Not the product
- not general SEO
- not ad management
- not generic website design
- not guaranteed ranking in ChatGPT/Gemini/Google AI features

---

## Core narrow hypothesis
> Korean laser-hair-removal clinics may pay for a small concierge service that creates a provider-approved canonical information layer from their existing official URLs, **if** they believe this reduces distortion in AI/search-driven comparison.

---

## Hypotheses to test tomorrow

### H1 — Problem hypothesis
These clinics care about this problem enough to respond.

More precise version:
- They care that customers compare clinics using AI/search.
- They care that branch / price / package info may be omitted or distorted.

Pass signal:
- at least 1 of the first 2 targets replies with curiosity, not instant dismissal
- reply examples:
  - “무슨 방식인지 설명해달라”
  - “URL 보내면 되냐”
  - “시범으로 볼 수 있냐”

Fail signal:
- both treat it as irrelevant marketing spam

---

### H2 — Workflow hypothesis
A low-friction workflow is attractive.

More precise version:
- clinics prefer “send URLs, review draft” over filling forms

Pass signal:
- at least 1 target is willing to send 2–3 official URLs

Fail signal:
- they demand a long explanation, form, or meeting before even sharing URLs

---

### H3 — Demo hypothesis
A clinic-specific canonical fact packet is more persuasive than a pretty landing page.

More precise version:
- showing source extraction + normalized facts + AI-readable record will feel credible
- prettier design alone will not sell the idea

Pass signal:
- target reacts positively to the Muse-style demo packet
- phrases like:
  - “아 이런 식으로 정리하는 거구나”
  - “이건 우리도 필요할 수 있겠다”

Fail signal:
- target does not understand why this is different from a normal webpage summary

---

### H4 — Value hypothesis
The real sellable unit is not “AI-friendly page creation” but:
- source-grounded canonicalization
- provider approval
- public deployment
- discoverability tracking

Pass signal:
- when explained this way, the offer feels more concrete and less like generic AI/SEO fluff

Fail signal:
- target keeps collapsing it into “그냥 홈페이지 정리” or “SEO 대행” only

---

### H5 — Pricing hypothesis
If there is interest, a small pilot can be sold at low-to-mid KRW six figures.

Initial test range:
- KRW 150,000
- KRW 300,000

Pass signal:
- no visible price shock at 150k
- at least one target asks what is included

Fail signal:
- immediate “그건 내부에서 하면 된다” or “그 돈 주고는 안 한다” from a qualified target

---

## Why this is narrow enough
Tomorrow we are **not** deciding the final long-term company.
We are only testing:
> “Will 1 Korean clinic care enough to send URLs for a tiny pilot around AI/search accuracy?”

If yes, continue.
If no, refine or kill.

---

### Step 2
